About GrantScore Pro

Built by grant professionals who know what funders look for — and what makes proposals fail.

Our Mission

To help nonprofits submit stronger, more competitive grant proposals — with clarity, confidence, and data-driven insights. Grant writing is time-intensive, and organizations consistently submit proposals without knowing whether they're truly ready. GrantScore Pro gives you an objective readiness score and the exact steps to improve it — before you invest hours writing.

How Blended Scoring Works

Your final score reflects both how ready your organization is and how strong your actual application narrative is. A high-capacity organization with a weak narrative will score lower than one that has done the work to align both.

NC-First Funder Data

GrantScore Pro currently includes enhanced alignment data for North Carolina funders — including common foundation priorities, typical grant ranges, and deadline patterns. When you paste a funder's stated priorities, our model checks your application text against those priorities to calculate a funder alignment bonus.< /p>

National funder data expansion is underway. The core scoring model works for any nonprofit, anywhere.

Limitations

GrantScore Pro provides readiness indicators — not guaranteed outcomes. Your score reflects how well your proposal aligns with common funder evaluation criteria based on your inputs. It surfaces the same gaps funders typically flag, but it does not predict any specific funder's decision. Use it as a diagnostic and improvement tool, not a submission guarantee.

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about GrantScore Pro.

General
What is GrantScore Pro?
A readiness-scoring tool that evaluates your grant proposal across seven weighted dimensions and gives you a 100-point score plus actionable insights — before you submit.
Does this replace a grant writer?
No. It supports grant writers by identifying strengths and gaps before submission — so you invest writing time where it matters most.
What do I do with my score after I get it?
Follow the Readiness flagged sections below — it shows your highest-impact improvement actions ranked by scoring weight. Fix those gaps, then re-score before submitting.
Scoring & Methodology
How does the scoring system work?
Your score is a blend: 60% organizational readiness(rated via sliders) + 40% application text quality(word count, specificity markers, and funder keyword alignment). See the How It Works tab for full detail.
What are the seven dimensions?
Mission Alignment(25%), Organizational Capacity(20%), Track Record(18%), Budget Fit(15%), Narrative Strength(12%), Funder Relationship(6%), Compliance Readiness(4%).
How accurate is the score?
The score reflects how well your proposal aligns with common funder evaluation criteria. It's a readiness indicator, not a guarantee — but users consistently find it surfaces the same gaps funders flag in rejection letters.
Will improving my score improve my chances?
Addressing the gaps the score surfaces — mission alignment, capacity documentation, narrative specificity — are the same things funders consistently cite when explaining award decisions. Stronger on these dimensions = stronger application.
Data & Privacy
Is my application text stored or shared?
No. Application text entered in Step 3 is scored entirely in your browser. Nothing you paste there is transmitted to any server or third party.
Do you share my information with funders?
Never. Your organization and scoring data are never shared with funders, foundations, or any third party.
Can I request deletion of my data?
Yes. Email hello@grantscorepro.com with your request and we'll remove your record within 48 hours.
NC-First Funder Data
What does "NC-first" mean?
We currently include enhanced alignment data for North Carolina funders, including common foundation priorities, grant ranges, and deadline patterns. National expansion is underway.
Can I use this outside NC?
Absolutely. The core scoring model works for any nonprofit, anywhere. NC-specific funder alignment data is an enhancement on top of the universal scoring framework.
